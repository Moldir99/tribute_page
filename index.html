Martin Luther King Jr.

The man who changed lives

Biography

  • January 15, 1929 - King was born in Atlanta, Georgia, to the Reverend Martin Luther King Sr. and Alberta Williams King.
  • 1934 - King's father changed his and his son's name after Protestant Reforamtion leader, Martin Luther
  • 1942 - he became the youngest assistant manager of a newspaper delivery station for the Atlanta Journal.
  • 1948 - King graduated at the age of 19 from Morehouse with a B.A. in sociology
  • June 18, 1953 - King married Coretta Scott
  • 1954 - King was called as pastor of the Dexter Avenue Baptist Church in Montgomery, Alabama
  • June 5, 1955 - he recieved his Ph.D. degree in systematic theology at Boston University
  • 1955 - King's role in the bus boycott transformed him into a national figure and the best-known spokesman of the civil rights movement
  • September 20, 1958 - King was signing copies of his book Stride Toward Freedom in Blumstein's department store in Harlem when he narrowly escaped death
  • 1959 - King published a short book called The Measure of A Man
  • 1961 - In December, King and the SCLC became involved into Albany Movement of desegregation coalition
  • April 1963 - the SCLC(Southern Christian Leadership Conference) began a campaign against racial segregation and economic injustice in Birmingham, Alabama
  • August 28, 1963 - King, representing the SCLC, was among the leaders of the "Big Six" civil rights organizations who were instrumental in the organization of the March on Washington for Jobs and Freedom
  • March 1964 - King and the SCLC joined forces with Robert Hayling's then-controversial movement in St. Augustine, Florida
  • December 1964 - King and the SCLC joined forces with the Student Nonviolent Coordinating Committee (SNCC) in Selma, Alabama
  • February 6, 1964, - King delivered the inaugural speech of a lecture series initiated at the New School called "The American Race Crisis
  • 1965 - Selma voting rights movement and "Bloody Sunday"
  • 1966 - After several successes in the south, King, Bevel, and others in the civil rights organizations took the movement to the North, with Chicago as their first destination
  • April 4, 1967 - King delivered a speech titled "Beyond Vietnam: A Time to Break Silence." at the New York City Riverside Church
  • April 15, 1967 - ing participated and spoke at an anti-war march from Manhattan's Central Park to the United Nations
  • January 13, 1968 - King called for a large march on Washington against "one of history's most cruel and senseless wars
  • 1968 - King and the SCLC organized the "Poor People's Campaign" to address issues of economic justice
  • March 29, 1968 - King went to Memphis, Tennessee, in support of the black sanitary public works employees
  • April 3 - King addressed a rally and delivered his "I've Been to the Mountaintop" address at Mason Temple, the world headquarters of the Church of God in Christ
  • April 4, 1968 - King was fatally shot by James Earl Ray at 6:01 p.m.
  • April 7 President Lyndon B. Johnson declared a national day of mourning for the civil rights leader
